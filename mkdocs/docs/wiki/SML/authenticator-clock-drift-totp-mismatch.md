# Authenticator clock drift and stale enrollment causing MFA prompt loop

[← Back to categories](../../index.md)

## Description

Affected users experience a repeated multi-factor authentication (MFA) prompt loop when signing in to the SSO portal. After entering a valid password, the login flow advances to the MFA challenge step, but time-based one-time passcode (TOTP) codes generated by the authenticator app are rejected and the MFA prompt immediately reappears. The loop prevents access to federated SaaS applications such as Office 365, Salesforce, Workday, and Concur, effectively blocking all normal work that depends on single sign-on.

The issue has been observed on both mobile and desktop authenticator apps and across multiple device types, including iPhones running iOS 16 and Windows 10 laptops. Authenticator codes may appear to be generating normally on the device, giving no obvious visual indication that anything is wrong. Sign-in logs for affected accounts typically show entries such as "MFA challenge rejected" and "Authentication failed: invalid_session," confirming that the codes are not being accepted by the identity provider.

Reports have come from individual users as well as groups of users within the same team. In one instance, multiple members of a Sales group were affected simultaneously following an overnight maintenance window, while in other cases the issue appeared on a single account after a phone operating-system update. The common thread across all reports is that the login process stalls at the MFA step and cycles indefinitely without completing authentication.

!!! note "Reported variations"

    - Some users encountered the issue immediately after a phone operating-system update, even though the authenticator app itself was not reinstalled or reset.
    - In at least one case, manually correcting the device clock to automatic network time did not resolve the loop on its own; the MFA factor enrollment also required a reset and re-registration before sign-in could complete.
    - A group of Sales users were affected simultaneously after an overnight maintenance window, with stale factor enrollments (some over 14 months old) identified as a contributing factor alongside device clock skew.

## Affected environment

Distribution across 3 reported cases:

- **Operating system:** iOS 16.4 (33%), Windows 10 (33%), iOS 16 (user-reported) (33%)
- **Device / platform:** Okta SSO (67%), mobile (33%)
- **Team:** Marketing - North America (33%), Sales (33%), KnowledgeWorkers (33%)
- **Region:** us-east-1 (100%)

## Root cause

The authenticator app on the affected device generates time-based codes that are out of sync with the identity provider's server clock, causing the codes to be rejected at validation. In some cases the device clock had drifted by several minutes — enough to fall outside the accepted time window for codes. This clock drift is sometimes compounded by stale MFA factor enrollment records on the user's account, where the factor registration had not been updated in many months, making the enrollment more susceptible to validation mismatches after device or operating-system changes.

## Diagnostics

Steps used to confirm this root cause:

1. Reproduced the user's SSO login flow to verify whether MFA repeatedly prompted instead of completing sign-in.  
   *Expected:* User completes MFA once and reaches the application landing page.
2. Reviewed the user's MFA enrollment state and active Okta factor for stale registration or token mismatch indicators.  
   *Expected:* User has an active factor with no stale or disabled enrollment.
3. Compared the user's group membership and application access policy scope to rule out conditional access or assignment issues.  
   *Expected:* User belongs to the expected access group and policy scope.
